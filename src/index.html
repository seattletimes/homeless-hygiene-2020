Seattle has added new hygiene services for the more than 3,500 unsheltered people in the city, but those efforts have largely lagged behind the added services of four other cities with similarly sized unsheltered homeless populations.
Seattle has installed the fewest new hand-washing stations out of these six metros over the last six weeks. It was also one of the last to do so, installing the first new hand-washing stations on March 27.
Seattle has also installed some of the fewest new toilets, with approximately 3.9 new toilets per every 1,000 unsheltered homeless residents. While San Francisco has only installed five new portable toilets to date, it also had a pre-existing mobile “pit stop” program with sinks, sharps containers, dog waste receptacles and 41 toilets installed across 13 neighborhoods before the pandemic.
